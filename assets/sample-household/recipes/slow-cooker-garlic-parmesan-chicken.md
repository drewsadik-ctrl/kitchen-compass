## Ingredients
- 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 2 teaspoons garlic salt
- 2 teaspoons paprika
- 2 teaspoons pepper
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 6 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
- 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved or quartered depending on size
- 2 cups shredded Parmesan
- parsley, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. In a small bowl, mix the garlic salt, paprika, and pepper together.
2. Sprinkle half of the seasoning mixture on the chicken thighs.
3. In a medium skillet over medium-high heat, add 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down and sear for about 2 minutes, or until golden.
4. Combine the remaining seasoning mixture with the minced garlic and remaining olive oil.
5. Halve or quarter the baby potatoes depending on size.
6. Toss the potatoes with the garlic-seasoning-oil mixture and place them in the bottom of the slow cooker.
7. Add the chicken on top of the potatoes.
8. Cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.
9. Remove the chicken thighs and place them under the broiler for a few minutes until the skin gets crispy again.
10. Right before serving, sprinkle with the Parmesan cheese.
11. Top with parsley if using.
12. Serve.

## House Tweaks / Change Log
- Ingredients and instructions above reflect the current house version.
- House default uses the 2x version of the original recipe: 8 chicken thighs instead of 4.
- After slow cooking, remove the thighs and broil them at the end so the skin crisps back up before serving.
- Can also be made in a large roasting pan if serving more than 4 people.
